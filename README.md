# Mariangela Dametto

## Computational Structural Biochemist working at the interface of molecular simulation, structural biology, drug discovery, and biomedical data science.

## About me
I have experience in molecular dynamics, docking, QM/MM, and protein–ligand and protein–protein interaction analysis, along with scientific scripting in Python and Bash. My work focuses on applying computational methods to biologically and medically relevant problems, including biomolecular mechanisms, peptide and protein systems, and translational biomedical research.

## Main interests
- Molecular Dynamics
- Docking and structure-based drug discovery
- QM/MM and enzymatic mechanisms
- Protein–ligand and protein–protein interactions
- Scientific workflows in Python and Bash
- AI/ML for biomedical and clinical data

## Tools and technologies
AMBER, GROMACS, AutoDock, Vina, PyMOL, VMD, Python, Bash, Linux, HPC

## Current focus
- Computational modeling of biomolecular interactions
- Peptide and protein system analysis
- Simulation-driven approaches for drug discovery
- Integration of computational methods with experimental and biomedical data
